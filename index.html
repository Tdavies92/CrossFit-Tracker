Top 5 Priorities

⚡ #1 — Engine Under Load

Biggest ROI. Train aerobically in Open-specific contexts: barbell cycling under fatigue, gymnastics + cardio combos, 8–20 min sustained output. Running helps your base but doesn't train this specifically.

🔼 #2 — Overhead Efficiency + Jerk Confidence

Overhead instability is costing you in every OHS, jerk, push press and HSPU-heavy workout. Daily full-body mobility + deliberate jerk skill work compounds over 18 months.

🤸 #3 — TTB + Muscle-Up Volume

TTB at 5–8 reps and BMU/RMU at 2–5 will cap your score in any gymnastics-heavy Open workout. Needs structured exposure. Target by Block 2: TTB 12–15 unbroken, BMU/RMU 5–7 linked.

⚖️ #4 — Barbell Cycling Efficiency

Your strength doesn't need major development — it needs to translate to efficiency at moderate loads. Touch-and-go practice, breathing under load, cycling at 60–70% without redlining.

🏃 #5 — Burpee Pace + Sprint Metcons

Burpees are a pacing and breathing skill. Consistent sub-maximal practice builds economy. Also need more sprint metcon exposure (8–14 min) — your worst time domain.

The Dad Rule

When life gives you 3 days: 1) Wendler → 2) CF class → 3) Drop or shorten Day 3. Never sacrifice sleep for a session. 7h sleep beats a 5am workout on 5h, every time.

Fatigue & Warning Signs

🔴 Red — Reduce Load

  • Sleep <6h for 3+ nights
  • Resting HR up 8+ bpm
  • Zero motivation 3+ days
  • 2+ sessions performance drop
  • Joint pain (not DOMS)
  • Illness — skip entirely

🟡 Amber — Modify

  • High work stress: cut D3 intensity 20%
  • Lingering soreness: Wendler only
  • Flat warm-up: 80% of plan
  • Life stress high: sleep > training

Under-Recovery Fix

Hard class effort → next session 70% max. 40g protein within 45 min post-training. High-stress day: swap D3 metcon for skill + easy row. Week 8 deload is mandatory — it's when adaptation happens.

⚠️ Overhead — Monitor Amber

If overhead instability doesn't improve after 6–8 weeks of consistent practice and full-body mobility work, consider a session with a sports physio or CF coach for a technique check. It may be a movement pattern issue rather than a flexibility issue.
