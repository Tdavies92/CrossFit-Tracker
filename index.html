General Info
Philosophy · Structure · Priorities · Fatigue Rules
The Philosophy
Train to be a well-rounded athlete. The CrossFit Open is a byproduct — a test of how far you have come, not the only reason you train. Build a genuine aerobic engine, real gymnastics capacity, efficient barbell technique, and the ability to work in all time domains. The Open will reflect all of it naturally.

How Blocks Work

Each block is 8 weeks of programming. At the end of Week 8 you retest benchmarks, export your data, and the next block gets generated based on your actual progress. The programme adapts to you, not a fixed plan written before you have trained.

Your Starting Point (2025)

  • Open result: 72nd percentile (top 28%) — target: 90th percentile (top 10%)
  • Strength: Squat 160 / DL 190 / Bench 110 — already near top-10% level
  • Olympic lifting: C&J 110 / Snatch 90 — technique gap on jerk and squat snatch
  • Gymnastics: Pull-ups strong, TTB 5–8/set, BMU 2–5 linked, HS walk limited
  • Conditioning: Good long-duration base (Murph 45:30), weak under barbell load and in sprint domain
Weekly Structure

Day 1 — CrossFit Class · 60 min

Full class as programmed. Classes cover compound lifting stimulus — no extra strength work needed. Optional 5 min after: TTB sets, burpee drills, or BMU practice — always skippable.

Day 2 — Open Gym · ~55 min

20 min Oly skill: Rotating weekly — jerk, snatch, C&J cycling. Barbell efficiency that classes do not address.
15–20 min Gymnastics: Rotating — TTB, BMU, HS walk, rope climbs, deficit HSPU.
15 min Targeted piece: Short metcon, threshold interval, or benchmark. Escalates each block.

Day 3 — CrossFit Class · 60 min

Full class. Optional 5 min after: different focus to Day 1 optional. Always skippable.

Day 4 — Zone 2 Aerobic · 25–50 min

Run, bike, or row at fully conversational pace (HR below 145bpm). This is the session that builds the aerobic engine classes cannot provide. Duration builds through blocks, reduces in taper.

Optional Day 5

A third class or easy aerobic session when life allows. Never forced — the plan works on 4 days.

The Dad Rule

When life gives you 3 days: 1) Open gym → 2) CF class → 3) Drop the Z2 or shorten to 20 min walk. Never sacrifice sleep for a session.
Development Priorities

1 — Build a Real Aerobic Engine

Your biggest long-term return. Zone 2 work and sustained metcon capacity is what separates you from top-10%. Strength is already there — the engine is the gap.

2 — Overhead Efficiency & Jerk Confidence

Overhead instability costs you in every jerk, OHS, and HSPU workout. Deliberate weekly jerk and snatch practice compounds into a real advantage over 8-week blocks.

3 — TTB & Muscle-Up Volume

TTB at 5–8 reps and BMU at 2–5 linked caps your score in any gymnastics workout. Needs structured practice, not random class reps.

4 — Barbell Cycling Efficiency

Strength under load is there. Touch-and-go cycling, breathing patterns, and moderate-load efficiency is the skill gap that the open gym sessions target directly.

5 — Sprint Domain & Burpee Pace

8–14 min is your weakest time domain. Burpees are a pacing skill. Both get targeted in open gym sessions each block.
Fatigue & Warning Signs

Red — Reduce Load

  • Sleep under 6h for 3+ nights
  • Resting HR up 8+ bpm
  • Zero motivation 3+ days
  • Performance drops 2+ sessions
  • Joint pain (not DOMS)
  • Illness — skip entirely

Amber — Modify

  • High work stress: easy class only
  • Lingering soreness: skip targeted piece
  • Flat in warm-up: reduce intensity 20%
  • Life stress high: sleep beats training
